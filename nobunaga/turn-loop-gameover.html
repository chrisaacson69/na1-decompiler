Game Over

display_fullscreen_graphic_sequence $8003 — the win / lose sequence and restart.
① decompiler → C ② data-walk labels ③ bytecode-verified flow ④ emulator-probed formula

The season loop only ever exits one way: a phase sets the abort gate ai_turn_flags & 128 — either a daimyo has unified Japan, or the player has been wiped out. vm_bootstrap falls through to display_fullscreen_graphic_sequence, which plays the appropriate end screen, waits for a button, and returns — and the loop restarts at init_new_game_state for a fresh game. It’s almost pure presentation: graphics, music, and two strings. The one decision is victory vs defeat.

The steps

The wizard, in order — each step grounded in the $89A3 bytecode:

  1. $800BQuiet down. Silence all audio (syscall_audio_control(0,0)), refresh the UI.
  2. $8015Victory or defeat? Branch on count_6da2_set() — it counts the flagged entries in the per-fief array $6DA2. Flagged > 0 takes the victory path; 0 takes defeat. (The exact meaning of the $6DA2 flag is still open — it also gates the per-pass abort and AI targeting; flagged as a data-walk.)
  3. $8019Victory. Upload the triumph graphic (ppu_upload_block_wrap(…, $9054, bank 6) + the $9D04 full screen), then “Congratulations, my lord!” → “In the year %d” (current_game_year) → “You united Japan!”, with the victory fanfare (call_bank10_entry(5)).
  4. $811DDefeat. The defeat music (call_bank10_entry(6)), “Game Over.”, a 6×64-step palette-fade animation (the screen dims out), and the defeat graphic ($AF64).
  5. $81C5Wait & restart. Spin until a controller press (syscall_read_controller), then return. vm_bootstrap loops back to init_new_game_state — a new game.

Where the art lives

The victory and defeat full-screens are static CHR uploaded from bank 6 ($9D04 victory, $AF64 defeat) and blitted via the usual ppu_upload_block_wrap / ppu_copy_rect_wrap pair. They’re prime targets for the “render graphics bits from code” frontier — the same code-driven extraction that pulled the title and portrait art — so both screens could be rendered to PNG straight from ROM without ever reaching the ending in-game.

What it pinned down

Game-over is a discriminator, not a computation. The whole sequence is two presentation branches selected by one boolean (count_6da2_set over $6DA2). No formula — the interesting state was decided earlier (whoever set the abort bit); this phase just announces it.
$6DA2 is an unresolved per-fief flag array — queued for a data-walk. It’s the victory/defeat discriminator here, but it’s also read in 10+ AI / combat sites and gates the command-pass abort. That breadth means it deserves a real data-walk rather than a guessed name.
