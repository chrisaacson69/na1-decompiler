What it does

View (driver_view $A6C7) is the game’s intelligence command, and the widest of the non-econ commands — it’s a loop over several screens. View which fief? lets you inspect any province’s stats (effect_view_a); your own fiefs are free, but spying on an enemy costs 10 gold per look (no gold → You have no gold.). Spying is a contest: effect_view_d compares your daimyo against the target and, on a bad roll, Our spy was caught. (animation 14). From the same command you can list a daimyo’s vassals (View vassals, effect_view_b/c) and open the strategic map (map_helper_e5f2 / map_helper_af10 — the very same render path the Map command uses, branched on scenario_fief_count).

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $A716"View which fief?" — pick any province.
  2. $A6EEOwn fief: view its full stats free (effect_view_a).
  3. $A7AAEnemy fief: costs 10 gold to spy; debited on the attempt.
  4. $A80ASpy contest (effect_view_d): your daimyo vs the target + an RNG roll.
  5. $A843Caught → "Our spy was caught." (animation 14).
  6. $A743Map sub-screen — opens the strategic map (map_helper_e5f2/af10, the same renderer as the Map command).
The information hub — and a second door to the map. View bundles three things the other commands keep separate: province inspection, vassal lists, and the strategic map. Reading your own holdings is free; reading an enemy’s is espionage — 10 gold a look and a skill-vs-skill roll that can get your spy caught. The map sub-screen calls the identical map_helper_e5f2 path as the Map command (and the kernel $E694 standalone viewer), which is why all three share one render core — and why pulling that illustrated map from ROM is filed as its own task.
