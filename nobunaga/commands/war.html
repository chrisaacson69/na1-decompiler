War

What it does

War (driver_war $9850) is the gateway into the battle engine, and by design the command’s own logic is brief: it raises and dispatches an army, then steps aside. After a chain of preconditions (effect_war_combat_prep_a/b/c/d — you need an army, an adjacent valid target, and — if your daimyo is in the fief — his clearance to march), it asks Attack where? (allies are rejected: They are your allies.), then How many men? and How much rice will they take? — the army carries its own supply, which is the lever behind the rice-exhaustion siege. If your daimyo is present you may lead them personally (sets bit 7 of war_side_state_flag — he joins the battle, and its risk). The driver then debits the attacking fief (men, rice and gold), declares war — effect_war_a zeroes the two daimyo’s relations cells, breaking any pact — stages the force in the war_attacker_men/rice/gold + battle_defending_province globals, plays the march animation (ui_helper_e80c(13)), flags the turn loop to redispatch (ai_turn_loop_redispatch_flag = 1), and returns. Everything after that — the tactical map, daily damage, morale, pursuit and the commander-resolution loop — runs in the separate bank-2 combat engine.

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $985FPreconditions — daimyo clearance + army/target/adjacency checks (effect_war_combat_prep_a/b/c/d).
  2. $98A5"Attack where?" — select an adjacent enemy province (an ally → "They are your allies." and abort).
  3. $98F1Force size capped at min(gold, men) (quartered if already at war).
  4. $9909"How many men?" → war_attacker_men.
  5. $991C"How much rice will they take?" → war_attacker_rice (the army’s supply), capped at min(men, fief rice).
  6. $994AIf the daimyo is here: "Will you lead them personally?" → sets bit 7 of war_side_state_flag (he marches with the army).
  7. $9967Debit the attacking fief: gold, rice and men all subtracted.
  8. $9993Declare war + hand off: effect_war_a clears the relations cells; march animation 13; ai_turn_loop_redispatch_flag = 1 → the bank-2 engine takes over.
Deliberately a thin page — the depth is the engine, not the command. War’s own driver just raises a force, charges its supply, declares the war, and flips a redispatch flag; the actual fight — tactical map, the 4-quadrant {1,2,4,7} damage model, the up-to-31-day loop that ends on commander resolution (not attrition), pursuit, and post-battle ravage / arms capture / ownership transfer — all lives in the bank-2 combat engine, which gets its own treatment. Two things the command does own are strategically load-bearing: the rice-as-supply prompt (an attacker with too little rice loses the siege to starvation) and the lead-personally choice (your daimyo can win a battle outright — or die in it).
