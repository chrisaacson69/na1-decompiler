What it does

Grant (driver_grant $AF66) sets a province’s AI governance policy (province_ai_state). Gated on your daimyo being present, you pick the fief (Which fief?), the game asks What are your orders?, and you choose one of six stances from the menu. The chosen value (1–5) is written to province_ai_state[fief] ($B050); the game confirms It’s currently a <X> state. OK to…? (or It’s already a <X> state.), plays the animation (ui_helper_e80c(11)), and replies Lord, you are truly wise. State 0 = “Home fief” means you keep controlling it by hand; the other five hand the wheel to the AI with a focus. The AI’s per-fief command driver then issues that province’s commands each turn according to the policy.

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $AF73Daimyo-present gate ($6DA2).
  2. $AFA0Select the fief — "Which fief?" — then "What are your orders?".
  3. $AFCCThe six-option policy menu is drawn (from the $F7D4 name table).
  4. $AFFBsubmenu_prompt(6) → pick a policy; option 6 backs out.
  5. $B00BAlready that policy → "It’s already a <X> state."
  6. $B050Set province_ai_state[fief] = policy; animation 11; "Lord, you are truly wise."

The six governance policies

The menu, read straight from the name table effect_view_a_data_f7d4 ($F7D4 → strings at $F7E0…). Index = the stored province_ai_state value.

statePolicyWhat the AI prioritizes
0Home fiefyou keep direct manual control — not delegated
1IndustrialBuild — grow the town / gold-income engine
2MilitaryTrain & Hire — raise soldiers and skill
3Balancedspread effort across economy and army
4FarmingGrow / Dam — raise agricultural output & rice
5Directthe most hands-off / aggressive delegated mode

The same six labels appear in the View screen (effect_view_a reads the identical table), which is how you read back a delegated fief’s current policy. Delegation is the late-game tempo play: once you hold more fiefs than you have action-slots to micromanage, Grant lets the AI run the rear provinces on a sensible focus while you spend your four seasons on the frontier.

Genuinely a mechanic, not a UI screen. Grant writes province_ai_state, the very field the AI command driver reads each turn to decide that fief’s actions — and the same field the conquest/neutralize code resets (neutralize_fief forces it to −1). It is the player’s hook into the AI economy: hand a rear fief a Farming or Industrial policy and it develops itself for free, no slot spent.
