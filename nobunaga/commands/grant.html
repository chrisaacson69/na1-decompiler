What it does

Grant (driver_grant $AF66) sets a province’s AI governance policy (province_ai_state). Gated on your daimyo being present, you pick the fief (Which fief?), the game asks What are your orders?, and you choose one of six stances from the menu. The chosen value (1–5) is written to province_ai_state[fief] ($B050); the game confirms It’s currently a <X> state. OK to…? (or It’s already a <X> state.), plays the animation (ui_helper_e80c(11)), and replies Lord, you are truly wise. The state is read every turn by the per-fief command driver (ai_per_fief_command_driver $B89B), which switches on it to pick that fief’s auto-action. State 5 = “Direct” is the one that means you run it by hand (issue_province_command — your own capital starts here); state 0 = “Home” is the AI’s general-economy default (every conquered/AI fief sits here); states 1–4 delegate to a themed AI loop.

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $AF73Daimyo-present gate ($6DA2).
  2. $AFA0Select the fief — "Which fief?" — then "What are your orders?".
  3. $AFCCThe six-option policy menu is drawn (from the $F7D4 name table).
  4. $AFFBsubmenu_prompt(6) → pick a policy; option 6 backs out.
  5. $B00BAlready that policy → "It’s already a <X> state."
  6. $B050Set province_ai_state[fief] = policy; animation 11; "Lord, you are truly wise."

The six governance policies

Index = the stored province_ai_state value; the third column is the actual turn-engine handler the driver ($B89B) switches to for that fief each turn.

statePolicyTurn-engine handler → what the fief auto-does
0Homeai_econ_action_state0 → ai_econ_command_dispatch — the general RNG-weighted economy loop. The AI default: every conquered/AI fief sits here (it is NOT manual control).
1Industrialai_develop_town_handler — develops the town (the gold engine).
2Militaryai_state2_recruit_arm_train — tries to launch a WAR first (ai_try_war_attack), then recruits / arms / trains. An autonomous aggressor.
3Balancedai_econ_command_dispatch — the general economic dispatch.
4Farmingai_develop_dam_and_grow — Dam + Grow (rice / output).
5Directissue_province_command — YOU control it by hand. Your own capital starts in this state; it is the opposite of hands-off.

Per the SRAM census the AI never uses states 1–4 — every AI fief is 0 (Home), the human capital 5 (Direct). And the player can never have a Home fief: conquest inherits the attacker's state (ai_state(conquered) = ai_state(attacker) ? 5 : 0, $E194), and you always attack from a Direct/granted fief — so every conquest comes out Direct (5), to micro or Grant. Home (0) is strictly better than Balanced (3) (same cascade + the AI-only 10% stat boost / 25% arms bump / spring boon, no decay) — but it's an AI-only mode you can't reach. So states 1–4 are your only way to stop micro-ing a fief: Grant to focus — Farming a breadbasket, Industrial a gold mint, Military an autonomous aggressor — accepting the state-≠0 spring decay and no subsidies. All delegated fiefs auto-set their own tax (≈35–64%, re-randomised each turn).

Genuinely a mechanic, not a UI screen. Grant writes province_ai_state, the very field the AI command driver reads each turn to decide that fief’s actions — and the same field the conquest/neutralize code resets (neutralize_fief forces it to −1). It is the player’s hook into the AI economy: where the player gets one command per fief per season, an AI-run fief executes its whole cascade — recruit, arm, train, build — every turn. Since the player can’t reach Home (every conquest comes out Direct), Grant is your only way to buy back that action throughput: a delegated fief develops, and a Military one wages war, for free, no slot spent — at the cost of the spring decay and the Home subsidies you never had.
