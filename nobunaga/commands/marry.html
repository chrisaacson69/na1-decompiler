Lord Command · No. 8

Marry

Bind a rival house by marriage — pay a dowry to wed into it. The strongest diplomatic tie in the game, and the only command that paints a bride’s portrait.

What you see

The Marry animation, rendered from ROM bytes.

The wedding scene (id 16); the bride’s face is a randomly-rolled composite portrait (rng%22 + 53). FROM ROM

Produced headless by tools/run-animation.py 16, which runs the game’s own animation scripting VM (bank 14 $80AD, id 16 from the descriptor table at $AF80) and intercepts every CHR upload, palette write and sprite placement to reconstruct each frame — real NES palette.

What it does

Marry (driver_marry $9DC4) is a diplomacy command gated on your daimyo being present in the fief ($6DA2). You choose a target house (Which fief?); the game rolls a bride and draws her portrait (active_province_idx_copy = rng%22 + 53 → draw_daimyo_portrait), then names the dowry: Lord <you>: <him> wants <N> gold. Pay? Pay it (and hold the gold) and the marriage alliance is recorded (diplomacy_helper3 $9E6F), the wedding animation plays (ui_helper_e80c(16)), and you’re told Your bride-to-be has arrived. / Don’t you long to hear the pitter-patter… If the house refuses (They’ve refused.) your daimyo’s standing takes the snub — records +2, +3 (skill) and +4 (charisma) each drop by one.

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $9DD7Daimyo-present gate ($6DA2) — you must be in the fief to court.
  2. $9DEASelect the target house — "Which fief?".
  3. $9E22The bride is rolled — rng%22 + 53 picks a composite face, drawn via draw_daimyo_portrait.
  4. $9E51Prompt the dowry: "<him> wants <N> gold. Pay?"
  5. $9E6CGold check — your fief must hold the dowry.
  6. $9E6FWed: alliance recorded (diplomacy_helper3), dowry debited, animation 16, "Your bride-to-be has arrived."
  7. $9EC6If refused: "They’ve refused." and your daimyo loses 1 each from records +2 / +3 (skill) / +4 (charisma).
The only command that snubs you back. Most refusals just cost a turn; a rejected marriage proposal actually drains three of your daimyo’s own stats (records +2/+3/+4) — the public loss of face. The reward is the tightest diplomatic bond in the game (a marriage alliance, diplomacy_helper3), and a one-off bit of flavor: the bride gets a real, randomly generated portrait through the same composite face system the replacement-daimyo generator uses.
