What it does

Pact (driver_pact $9C4F) is a diplomacy command. You pick a target daimyo (Which fief?), and a relations helper (diplomacy_helper $9C9C) sets the gold he demands to agree. The game shows Lord <you>: <him> wants <N> gold. Pay? — answer yes (ui_helper_d3a7) and, if your selected fief holds the gold, it is debited and the alliance is recorded (diplomacy_helper2 $9CF3); the pact animation plays (ui_helper_e80c(3)) and you’re reminded War is inevitable, so don’t let your guard down. If you can’t (or won’t) pay, you get You have no gold. or — if he refuses — They’ve refused. / Who needs him anyway. No formula: the cost is set by the diplomacy state, and the only effect is gold spent + a relation flipped.

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $9C6ESelect Pact; pick the target — "Which fief?".
  2. $9C9CA diplomacy helper sets the gold the target daimyo demands to ally.
  3. $9CBCPrompt "Lord <you>: <him> wants <N> gold. Pay?"
  4. $9CCAGold check. Your fief lacks the gold → "You have no gold."
  5. $9CE2Pay: gold is debited and the alliance recorded (diplomacy_helper2).
  6. $9CF7Animation (ui_helper_e80c(3)), then "War is inevitable, so don’t let your guard down."
  7. $9D19If he refuses: "They’ve refused." / "Who needs him anyway." — no gold spent.
A diplomacy command, not an econ formula. The price isn’t a curve you can solve — it’s read out of the relations state by diplomacy_helper, and the whole effect is gold spent → relation set. That’s why Pact gets a functional page: the depth is in the diplomacy/relations subsystem (the relations matrix the AI decays each turn), not in Pact itself.
