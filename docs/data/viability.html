Starting-Fief Viability — who survives the opening

Which clans are doomed from turn one? The engine's invade decision is a pure men-count ratio (fief_men_ratio_pct = 100·men(atk)/(men(atk)+men(def)), $949A) — not the 8-stat combat strength, which only resolves who wins a battle once started. Derived from the bytecode; every number below is computed from the scenario's starting stats.

AI attack? — will this fief invade its weakest neighbour under the AI-vs-AI gate (share > ~70% ≈ 2.3× the target). INVADES = certain, invades? = rng band.
AI defense / m_safe — m_safe is the men level at which no neighbour clears 2.3× you; VULNERABLE = below it now, revolt→VULN = a low-morale revolt (men /2) drops you under it.
human threats / playable? — against a human the AI uses the harsher grudge gate (share > 47% ≈ only 0.89× you). But a neighbour is only a real threat if it ALSO clears the stage-2 logistics gate ($9046): its gold/rice-capped attack_budget = min(2·rice, men, gold) must cover your min(rice,men), or it aborts at commit. And the AI only attacks its WEAKEST neighbour (pick_weakest_men_fief), so a neighbour threatens you only if you are its weakest — strong fiefs that aren't anyone's weakest are ignored. "human threats" counts neighbours that target you AND clear both gates: UNPLAYABLE = 3+, hard = 1–2, PLAYABLE = 0. (DRIVE/relations skip-gate never fires at start — every daimyo has drive ≥ 50.)
revolt risk — a PLAYABLE start can still die by RNG: a troop revolt halves men (MEN/2) and may open a threat absent at full strength. Revolt eligibility is exact ($9E21): a fief qualifies when min(morale,50)² < rng(2025) — impossible at morale ≥ 45, then rising as (2024−morale²)/2025 ("elig" shown). The per-season chance is that × the ~75% uprising branch × handler/variant constants (a low-charisma daimyo adds a second path). Noto is the case — safe at 48 men, but morale 23 (≈74% eligible).

tier / assessment is the human-play read — it layers the long game (economy catch-up, the player's character re-roll, buffering) on top of the turn-1 mechanics the other columns compute. Rows are tier-sorted.
