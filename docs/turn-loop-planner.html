The Season Tick

ai_strategic_turn_planner $A455 — turn setup and the random-event engine.
① decompiler → C ② data-walk labels ③ bytecode-verified flow ④ emulator-probed formula

Before any daimyo picks a command, the whole world advances one season. ai_strategic_turn_planner is that tick: it saves, rolls the calendar forward, re-prices the market, runs the per-fief economy update (drift, the harvest, aging and death), and then fires the random-event engine — floods, earthquakes, riots, revolts. It’s the densest routine in the game, and walking it turned up two long-standing unknowns: what $6D2D is, and what the disaster events actually do.

The steps

The wizard, in order — each step grounded in the $89A3 bytecode:

  1. $A45BSave & UI. Show the strategic map; if a save is pending, write_sram_save_checksum_and_signature commits SRAM (a checksum over the live regions — the game saves at season end).
  2. $A47FCalendar. current_season = (season+1)&3; the year ticks only when that wraps to 0 — four seasons to a year.
  3. $A495Re-price the market. roll_period_rate_table_6e0b re-rolls the five market rates, so buy-low / sell-high timing genuinely matters period to period.
  4. $A498The per-fief economy update (per_period_fief_daimyo_update_driver $A30D) — drift, the season-phased work (incl. the harvest), and the death sweep. See below.
  5. $A4C3The random-event engine. Pick an event family, roll each fief for eligibility, then apply the disaster / riot / revolt to the affected provinces. See below.
  6. $A55FIllness pass. Each daimyo (health ≤ 99, not resting) is weakness-marked with probability (100 − health) / 400; a freshly-marked one gets “… has taken ill.”

Turn setup — the per-fief economy update

per_period_fief_daimyo_update_driver $A30D sweeps every fief, then does work that depends on the season:

  • Daimyo luck drifts. drift_daimyo_luck nudges each daimyo’s Luck stat (record +3) by rng%11 − 5 = −5…+5 every season — a literal random walk, which is exactly why a daimyo’s fortunes swing from year to year.
  • Tax drives loyalty. A fief whose fief_tax_rate ($6D2D) is below 90 − skill has its loyalty multiplied by 0.9 that season. (Direction reads counterintuitively — flagged for a bytecode recheck — but the dependency on the tax rate is real.)

Then one season-specific job, on top of the universal aging + natural-death roll:

current_seasonThe season’s job
0— (aging + death only)
1Reset the economic high-water marks
2HARVEST — gold & rice income = tax% × economic base
3Relations-matrix decay

The big $6D2D find lives here: it’s the per-fief tax rate (driver_tax writes the player’s 1–100 input straight into it), and the harvest reads it as the income multiplier — income = pct_op(tax, wealth) + pct_op(tax, 40%·town). So “raise tax” literally scales every fief’s gold/rice yield.

The random-event engine

Each season the planner may fire a world event. First it selects a family (season-gated and RNG-weighted — floods skew to one season, the political events to others), then it rolls every fief for eligibility: square_over_2025_probability_roll compares a stat² against rng%2025 (the stat capped at 50), tested against loyalty, (100 − tax), and the daimyo’s charisma. Low loyalty, high tax, and weak leadership are what make a province a target. The eligible fiefs go on a list, and the handler hits each one:

EventAnimWhat it does to each affected fief
Flood20wealth −1…4; output cut to (0.9 × dams)% — so dams are flood insurance: a maxed fief keeps ~90% of output, an undammed one loses almost all
Quake / plague31men ×(50–99)% and output ×(50–99)%; if the daimyo is present in the fief, a health hit + weakness mark on top
Riot23unrest across high-tension provinces (no ownership change)
Revolt25revolt_spread_sweep_flip_fief_ownership — a fief changes hands to the rebels; the harshest outcome
Boon—event_boost_province_wealth_loyalty: wealth + (rng%10 + charisma/10), loyalty up — the rare good news

Every event has a from-ROM animation (the ids above) pullable with tools/run-animation.py. The exact family-selection probabilities live in goto-heavy bytecode and are the next depth increment — but the effects above are read straight from the handlers.

What it pinned down

$6D2D is the per-fief tax rate — confirmed. driver_tax displays it as “Tax is %d” and writes the player’s 1–100 input into it; the harvest reads it as the income multiplier. The old harvest-formula “od_pct” was this. Labeled fief_tax_rate (regen-guard clean).
Dams are flood insurance. Flood output loss is (0.9 × dams)% retained — a third strategic reason to build dams, alongside rice yield and the Dam-vs-Grow crossover. Undammed fiefs are wiped by a single flood.
Daimyo Luck is a random walk. Every season each daimyo’s Luck (record +3) drifts −5…+5 — so a rival’s fortunes genuinely wander over a campaign, and the field-+3 swings seen across save dumps are just this walk, not a hidden depletion mechanic. The 7 stats: Age, Health, Drive, Luck, Charisma, IQ, + a portrait flag.
Events target the disloyal, over-taxed, and poorly-led. Eligibility rolls on loyalty, (100 − tax), and charisma — the same three levers you manage with Give, Tax, and which daimyo governs. The event system is the feedback loop on bad governance.
