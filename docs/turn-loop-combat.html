The Combat Engine

battle_init_driver $AFE1 (bank 2) — deploy, fight 31 days, resolve.
① decompiler → C ② data-walk labels ③ bytecode-verified flow ④ emulator-probed formula

When a war is declared or an uprising spawns, the season loop hands control to the bank-2 battle engine. battle_init_driver $AFE1 is the whole battle — not a per-day step, as older notes had it: it loads the tactical map, deploys both armies once, then runs a 31-day loop of movement and fighting, and exits through a single resolution handler. Re-reading it against the now-clean decompile overturned the project’s long-standing “4-quadrant {1,2,4,7} damage formula” — that turned out not to be damage at all (below) — and resolved the real model into three decoupled mechanisms plus a four-way terminator. The combat math is now fully decoded.

The steps

One battle, end to end — every stage grounded in the bank-2 bytecode of battle_init_driver $AFE1:

  1. $AFE1Battle init. Set input mode, load the defender’s tactical map (map_populate), render the arena, clear the per-fief battle fields.
  2. $9B08Deploy both armies — once. deploy_both_sides_units_loop: each side’s men are split into 5 unit-type slots ($6FBC) by the province’s composition table $76A9 (set by the Assign command); then set_combat_arena_rect_by_approach $9675 picks the attacker’s entry edge from the compass bearing between the two fiefs on the strategic map, and units are placed.
  3. $B05BDay loop top. “Day %2d”. The loop counter runs 1 → 31.
  4. $B06DRice check (per side). A signed rice ≤ 0 test on unit_record+2; if a side is out of rice the battle ends here → resolution code 4 (“out of rice”).
  5. $ADD6Both sides act. run_both_sides_combat_turn: each side (AI or player) moves every unit and resolves attacks — kills and captures happen here. A decisive result (commander down / annihilation) returns its outcome code and the battle ends.
  6. $B09FDaily rice drain. consume_daily_battle_rice: rice −= live_men / 30 (+ carried remainder) for each side — the supply clock.
  7. $B0B8Day 31. If neither side broke first, the loop exhausts → resolution code 3 (“your men are exhausted!”).

What we thought it was — and what it is

For a year the project carried a “combat damage formula”: sub $9675 reading two attribute tables, a four-quadrant branch, and damage magnitudes {1,2,4,7}. Reading the clean code decompile — and confirming on the emulator (tools/probe-combat-arena.py) — overturned all of it. $9675 (now set_combat_arena_rect_by_approach) computes no damage. The “attribute tables” were strategic_map_fief_x/y (fief coordinates); the “magnitudes” were the corners of a rectangle; and the function only ever returns an edge code, never a number that hits a unit. The four words $6FF6–$6FFC are the tactical arena’s bounding box, and the very next routine (is_coord_in_combat_rect) reads them as exactly that. A clean lesson in re-verifying a “solved” result after the toolchain improves.

Deployment: the strategic map decides your edge

The default arena is the full 11×5 grid (0,0)–(10,4). set_combat_arena_rect_by_approach shrinks it to the strip the attacker enters from, chosen by the compass bearing between the two fiefs’ overworld coordinates — so where you attack from on the map decides where your units start in the battle:

Approach (atk vs def)Arena rectEntry stripedge code
Eastx ∈ [7,10]east edge4
Westx ∈ [0,3]west edge3
Northy ∈ [0,2]north edge1
Southy ∈ [2,4]south edge2

The per-side battle record $6F7D + 6·side is a {gold, rice, men} triple (the “Gold/Rice/Men” display reads it): +0 gold, +2 rice, +4 men.

Three ways to lose a soldier

Combat damage is not one number — it’s three decoupled mechanisms:

1 · The rice/supply clock (consume_daily_battle_rice $830B). The legendary “/30”: each day an army of men eats men/30 rice. Starting rice ≈ men, so it empties at ~day 30 — the real basis of the “31-day limit” and the rice-exhaustion / doughnut-fief defence.

rice(+2) −= live_men(+4) / 30        # per day, + carried remainder

Cross-dump proof (tools/probe-combat-bleed.py): between two saves of one battle, rice drained men-proportionally (−12 and −8 = the 32:21 men ratio) while men and the visible slot strengths held constant — a supply drain, not a kill.

2 · Kills (apply_pct_reduction_to_unit_strength $9D03). When two units clash, 100% of the damage is split between them by relative strength:

share = math32_2arg(A, B) = 100·A / (A+B)
defender loses share% of its slot · attacker loses (100−share)%

3 · Capture (resolve_attack_apply_casualties $9E73) — a morale + charisma contest, emulator-confirmed (tools/probe-combat-casualty.py):

atk = rng(6−skill) + morale(atk) + charisma(atk daimyo)
def =               morale(def) + charisma(def daimyo) + 2·skill
if atk > def:  captured = pct_op( math32_2arg(atk, atk−def), enemy_slot_strength )
               # those men DEFECT to the attacker’s slot 0 (cap 9999)

So a winning attack converts enemy soldiers rather than only killing them — and a strong leader’s charisma can win battles outright: in a real Oda matchup the attacker prevailed at every morale from 40 to 120 on charisma alone.

How a battle ends

The 31-day loop exits to dispatch_battle_resolution $AF3B with an outcome code that selects the end message. There are four conditions plus retreat:

codeConditionMessage
3day-31 timeout“your men are exhausted!”
4rice ≤ 0“supply reports… out of rice”
5commander (unit type 0) killed“the command unit has been destroyed!”
6annihilation (5 with no units left)“not a single soldier remains”
—Flee command“%s has retreated”

Unit type 0 is the commander — check_commander_alive_both_sides runs after every single unit action, so losing it ends the battle instantly. (The familiar “You have no rice/soldiers!” strings are the strategic pre-battle affordability checks in bank 1, not these tactical terminators.)

The aftermath: spoils, pillage, conquest

On an attacker win, apply_conquest_outcome $E03C settles the province:

StepEffect
Spoilsthe conquered fief absorbs both sides’ surviving gold + rice + men (atk + def) — the capture mechanic’s payoff, realised at the strategic layer
Pillageoutput −= pct_op(rng(10)+10, output) — a random 10–19% output hit
Ownershiparms + daimyo transferred; the defeated daimyo’s other fiefs are neutralised (province_ai_state = −1); a new daimyo installed if eliminated — the empire is dismantled in one resolution

What each daimyo stat does in battle

The stats split into two roles: charisma drives the damage; the rest drive the AI’s decision to engage. The engagement score (ai_sum_battle_strength) is a weighted attacker-vs-defender tally — for each stat the side that wins it adds that weight (battle_strength_stat_weights $B5B1):

StatfieldDamage roleAI-score weight
Health+1—5
Drive+2—10
Luck+3—10

skill (const_two)—rng(6−skill), +2·skill(province) 25
moraleprov +18contest term10

So IQ most influences whether the AI attacks, while charisma decides how the battle goes once joined — and province skill (weight 25) is the single biggest term.

What it pinned down

The “4-quadrant damage formula” was the deployment rectangle. $9675 sets the tactical arena’s bounding box ($6FF6–$6FFC) to the attacker’s entry edge by compass approach; it returns an edge code, never damage. Oracle-confirmed — the {1,2,4,7} “magnitudes” were rectangle corners.
The legendary “/30” is the rice clock, not a damage divisor. consume_daily_battle_rice drains men/30 rice per day. It unifies the “/30”, the 31-day limit, the “out of rice” terminator, and the rice-exhaustion strategy into one routine — cross-dump proven men-proportional, decoupled from kills.
Combat converts soldiers, and charisma can win outright. The attack is a morale + charisma contest; the winner’s margin captures enemy men to its own side. A strong leader’s charisma can win regardless of morale — the decisive combat stat.
Unit type 0 is the commander; losing it ends the battle. check_commander_alive_both_sides runs after every unit action; an absent type-0 unit triggers the code-5 “command unit destroyed” terminator.
