Marry

Bind a rival house by marriage — pay a dowry to wed into it. The strongest diplomatic tie in the game, and the only command that paints a bride’s portrait.
① decompiler → C ④ animation VM → ROM render ⚠ subsystem entry point

What you see

The Marry animation, rendered from ROM bytes.

The wedding scene (id 16); the bride’s face is a randomly-rolled composite portrait (rng%22 + 53). FROM ROM

Produced headless by tools/run-animation.py 16, which runs the game’s own animation scripting VM (bank 14 $80AD, id 16 from the descriptor table at $AF80) and intercepts every CHR upload, palette write and sprite placement to reconstruct each frame — real NES palette.

What it does

Marry (driver_marry $9DC4) is a diplomacy command gated on your daimyo being present in the fief ($6DA2). You choose a target house (Which fief?); the game rolls a bride and draws her portrait (active_province_idx_copy = rng%22 + 53 → draw_daimyo_portrait), then names the dowry: Lord <you>: <him> wants <N> gold. Pay? Pay it (and hold the gold) and the marriage alliance is recorded (diplomacy_helper3 $9E6F), the wedding animation plays (ui_helper_e80c(16)), and you’re told Your bride-to-be has arrived. / Don’t you long to hear the pitter-patter… The dowry (vs an AI house) is sized to your treasury — ~50–79% of your gold + 200, needing gold > 200 — and is paid to the rival, recording the strongest tie (relation 90). Each attempt costs −1 Drive; if the house refuses (They’ve refused.) your daimyo permanently loses one each from Drive (+2), Luck (+3) and Charisma (+4).

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $9DD7Daimyo-present gate ($6DA2) — you must be in the fief to court.
  2. $9DEASelect the target house — "Which fief?".
  3. $9E22The bride is rolled — rng%22 + 53 picks a composite face, drawn via draw_daimyo_portrait.
  4. $9E51Dowry (vs an AI house) = ~50–79% of YOUR gold + 200 (floor: gold > 200, offered ~1-in-skill): "<him> wants <N> gold. Pay?"
  5. $9E6CGold check — your fief must hold the dowry.
  6. $9E6FWed: dowry moved to the rival, relation set to 90 (set_marriage_relation), animation 16, "Your bride-to-be has arrived."
  7. $9EC6If refused: "They’ve refused." and your daimyo permanently loses 1 each from Drive (+2), Luck (+3), Charisma (+4).
The only command that snubs you back. Most refusals just cost a turn; a rejected marriage proposal permanently drains three of your daimyo’s core stats — Drive (+2), Luck (+3), Charisma (+4) each by one (the public loss of face). And like Pact, the dowry is sized to your own treasury and handed to the rival, for the tightest bond in the game (relation 90). One-off flavor: the bride gets a real, randomly generated composite portrait.
