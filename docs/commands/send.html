Lord Command · No. 4

Send

Ship rice and gold to one of your other provinces. Zero attrition — the only limit is the receiver’s headroom. The snowball engine.

In game

The Send animation, rendered purely from ROM bytes — no emulator capture.

A porter hauls a cart of koku and goods overland to the receiving province. FROM ROM

When to use it

Send from a maxed donor whose output you can no longer use — those fiefs are worth more as pumps than as producers. Doctrine: when a donor’s surplus exceeds ~3–4× a recipient’s, ship it; when the donor is capped, always ship.

Changes: target rice/gold +amount · source rice/gold −amount (1:1, no attrition).
Under the hood — the verified bytecode

How it works

Driver $9A5D reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $9A67Select Send, then the destination — "Send where?"
  2. $9A9CRice capacity = min(target.header − target.rice, source rice available).
  3. $9AB2Prompt "Rice" → number entry (capped at capacity). Zero capacity → "We’re at our limit."
  4. $9AE6Gold capacity = min(target.header − target.gold, source gold available).
  5. $9AFBPrompt "Gold" → number entry (capped at capacity).
  6. $9B34"Is this OK?" confirmation.
  7. $9B41Apply: target gold/rice += amounts; source gold/rice −= amounts — exactly 1:1.
  8. $9B6AThe animation plays — ui_helper_e80c(17) hands id 17 to the bank-14 animation VM.
  9. $9B70"The supplies have arrived safely." → confirm.

The exact formula

Read straight from $8BE5 bytecode and execution-verified.

// per resource (rice, then gold) — capacity helper effect_send ($8BE5)
capacity = min( target.header − target.field,   // receiver headroom
                source.available )      // can’t send what you lack
amount   = number_input(cap = capacity)

// applied at confirm — NO attrition, exactly 1:1
target.rice += rice_amt;   source.rice −= rice_amt
target.gold += gold_amt;   source.gold −= gold_amt
The snowball engine. Two properties make Send the backbone of a runaway economy: (1) zero attrition — every koku and gold piece arrives — and (2) the only limit is the receiver’s headroom (header − current). A maxed-out fief can therefore act as a pure pump, shovelling its whole surplus to the frontier each turn at no loss. Pair it with Give (which converts shipped rice into loyalty/morale) and Grow/Build on the receiving end. Send moves rice and gold in one command — two capacity checks, two number entries, one cart.

Validated against the ROM

effect_send caps each transfer at the receiver’s headroom and the sender’s stock — min(header − current, available). No attrition.
