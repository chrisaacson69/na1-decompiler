Bribe

Pay a spy to bribe an enemy fief’s peasants into defecting to you — the gold-for-spy command. A loyalty/charisma contest, not a sabotage menu.

In game

The Bribe animation, rendered from ROM bytes.

A spy (blue) approaches a banner-waving peasant — the defection scene (id 26). A thin 2-frame clip (the un-seeded-CHR caveat the develop animations also hit). FROM ROM

What it does

Bribe (driver_bribe $AAAE → effect_bribe $8D4D) is the separate espionage command — distinct from Hire▸Ninja’s sabotage menu (see Hire ▸ Ninja). You need more than 10 gold; you pick a target fief (Bribe which?) and answer Gold for spy? with how much to spend. A contest (bribe_success_check $8D02) then decides the outcome. On success a slice of the target’s output — its peasant/agricultural base — defects to your fief (%d peasants have defected); on failure (No peasants defected!) your daimyo loses a charisma point and part of the spent gold is forfeit. The number of peasants moved is loyalty − (⌊(30 + rng%25) × (min(loyalty, √gold) + 1) ÷ 100⌋ + 1) — it scales with the target’s loyalty (emulator: loyalty 80 → 63–76, loyalty 40 → 23–30, loyalty 10 → 4–6), not a flat percentage of output (compute_bribe_effect_value $8BC1, emulator-confirmed).

The contest — who wins

The success roll, read straight from bribe_success_check bytecode ($8D3D SCMPGT → $8D3E JUMPF).

OutcomeConditionEffect on the fiefs
Successyour(loyalty + daimyo charisma) > target(loyalty + charisma) + rng%10, then a 50% coin flippeasants (output) transfer to your fief: loyalty − (⌊(30+rng%25)·(min(loyalty,√gold)+1)/100⌋ + 1)
Failureotherwisenothing transfers; your daimyo −1 charisma; part of the spent gold is forfeit

The win condition is the intuitive one — a loyal, charismatic lord bribes a weaker enemy fief’s peasants away. The direction was settled from the bytecode: bribe_success_check compares local11 (built from the LAST-pushed arg = your fief) against local10 (the target + rng), and the VM passes call args into frame slots in reverse push order (the same convention probe-math32 documented). The $8BD0 DEREF of that same slot — only the pointer arg can be dereferenced — independently confirms the reverse mapping. (Field identities: loyalty@+12, daimyo charisma@+4.)

Where it goes

This command hands off to a bigger subsystem; the depth lives there.

🕳️ The spy-gold quirk. the closed form is settled, but it has a counter-intuitive twist: more spy gold raises √gold, which raises the subtracted resistance (until √gold ≥ loyalty) — so minimal gold maximizes the peasant transfer. Bytecode + emulator agree; worth an in-game sanity check that gold isn’t buying something else (success odds are gold-independent in $8D02).
Under the hood — the verified bytecode

How it works

Driver $AAAE reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $AAC2Gold gate. Need gold > 10, else "You have no gold."
  2. $AAD8Prompt "Bribe which?" — pick the target province.
  3. $AAF6Prompt "Gold for spy?" → number entry (your gold, less the base stake).
  4. $8D74The contest (bribe_success_check $8D02) — see the table below.
  5. $8D9DOn success the defection animation plays — ui_helper_e80c(26) — and a slice of the target’s output moves to your fief: "%d peasants have defected."
  6. $8DBFOn failure → "No peasants defected!", daimyo −1 charisma, part of the stake forfeit.
Bribe is NOT the sabotage menu. An earlier note filed the Uprising/Revolt/Dams/Assassin/Arson menu under “Bribe”; the bytecode shows that menu belongs to Hire▸Ninja (ninja.html). Bribe ($8D4D) is its own, simpler thing: gold-for-spy peasant defection — one contested effect that steals a chunk of the target’s peasant output. Both are gold-funded espionage, which is why they blurred together; the code keeps them firmly apart.
