Lord Command · No. 16

Assign

Reorganize a garrison — interactively reallocate its soldiers across the unit types. An editor screen, not a one-shot effect. Costs a flat 30 gold.

What it does

Assign (driver_assign $AD67) opens an interactive arms-allocation editor for the selected province. It requires soldiers (else You have no soldiers.) and a flat 30 gold (else You have no gold.), which is debited up front. effect_assign ($AC11) then draws the arms-edit screen (render_arms_edit_screen over the province’s arms region at $7017) and runs an input loop: the d-pad moves a cursor across the rows and adjusts each, A/B (poll_input cases) commit or cancel. The five rows are the army’s unit-type composition — the foot / cavalry / musket mix that feeds straight into the combat damage model (different unit types carry different damage multipliers). Unlike the econ commands there is no formula and no animation: it’s a UI editor that writes the arms fields directly.

Under the hood — the verified bytecode

How it works

Driver $AD67 reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $AD7DSoldier check — no men → "You have no soldiers."
  2. $AD91Gold check — a flat 30 gold is required and debited.
  3. $AC26effect_assign draws the arms-edit screen over $7017 (the province arms region).
  4. $AC36Input loop: d-pad moves the cursor over the five unit-type rows and adjusts the allocation; A/B commit or cancel.
The army-composition command. Where Train raises skill and Hire adds bodies, Assign decides what kind of soldiers they are — the foot/cavalry/musket split that the combat engine multiplies into damage (foot ×1, cavalry ×2, musket ×3 in the damage model). It’s an editor rather than a formula, which is why it gets a functional page: the depth is the combat model the composition feeds, not Assign itself. The flat 30-gold fee is a small reorganization tax — cheap, but it still spends one of your four action-slots.
