Pact

Pay gold to a rival daimyo to broker an alliance — a diplomatic command, not an economic one. He names the price; you pay or you don’t.

What it does

Pact (driver_pact $9C4F) buys peace from a rival. You pick a target daimyo (Which fief?), and the AI names a price (prompt_diplomacy_pact $E3A4) sized to your own treasury: pct_op(yourGold, 50) + pct_op(yourGold, rng%50) + 20 — roughly half to all of your gold, plus 20. The AI only offers a pact about 1-in-skill attempts, and refuses a militarily-weak lord two times in three. The game shows Lord <you>: <him> wants <N> gold. Pay? — answer yes and, if your fief holds the gold, it is handed to the rival (your.gold −= N; target.gold += N) and the alliance is recorded at relation 70 (set_pact_relation $9CF3); the pact animation plays and you’re reminded War is inevitable, so don’t let your guard down. If you can’t/won’t pay: You have no gold. / Who needs him anyway.; if he refuses: They’ve refused. Every attempt also costs your daimyo −1 Drive (record +2), and −2 if you back out or are refused.

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $9C6ESelect Pact; pick the target — "Which fief?".
  2. $9C93−1 Drive (daimyo record +2) just for opening negotiations.
  3. $9C9CThe AI names a price = ~50–99% of YOUR gold + 20 (offered ~1-in-skill; a weak lord is refused 2/3).
  4. $9CBCPrompt "Lord <you>: <him> wants <N> gold. Pay?"
  5. $9CCAGold check. Your fief lacks the gold → "You have no gold."
  6. $9CE2Pay: gold is moved to the rival and relation set to 70 (set_pact_relation $9CF3).
  7. $9CF7Animation (ui_helper_e80c(3)), then "War is inevitable, so don’t let your guard down."
  8. $9D19If he refuses: "They’ve refused." / "Who needs him anyway." — no gold spent.
Peace you can’t afford when you’d want it. The price is a curve — but on your own treasury, not the rival’s: the richer you are, the more a pact costs, and the gold goes straight into your enemy’s coffers. The AI offers it only about 1-in-skill attempts and refuses the weak outright, so the lord who most needs a truce is the least likely to be sold one. The only product is a relation number (70) in the matrix the AI decays each turn — though because the price tracks your current gold, a near-empty treasury buys a pact for ~20, so whether cheap diplomacy can rival the faction-ending assassination is an open synthesis question.
