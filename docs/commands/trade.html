What it does

Trade (driver_trade $A1B6) is the game’s merchant subsystem — by far the richest of the non-combat commands. It is the one command that may not be available on a given turn: it opens with a presence check (effect_trade $8A15) and, if it fails, you only get No merchant in the area. The merchant is always in the two commercial capitals — fief 13 (Kyoto / Yamashiro) and 14 (Sakai / Settsu) — and everywhere else only when a per-turn flag bit is set (ai_turn_flags & 1), so roughly half your turns elsewhere have no merchant. When he is present you’re greeted with Lord, how may I serve you? and a six-option menu; each completed deal plays animation 0 and offers Let’s do business again. Every price comes from the market rate table (loan_rate / gold_rice_exchange_rate / arms_buy_price_rate at $6E0B…), which re-rolls every turn — so when and where you trade is itself a lever.

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $A1BEMerchant-presence gate (effect_trade $8A15): fief 13/14 always pass; else ai_turn_flags & 1. Fail → "No merchant in the area."
  2. $A1C8The six-option menu is drawn (labels from $FF4F: Loan / Repay / Sell / Buy / Arms / Menu).
  3. $A1F3"Lord, how may I serve you?" → submenu_prompt(6) dispatches through jumptab_b9dc.
  4. $A216On a completed deal: animation 0 + "Let’s do business again." (the menu loops).
  5. $A23APick Menu → "Bye." and exit.

The six services

Read from jumptab_b9dc ($B9DC → $9F04/$9FAF/$A003/$A068/$A113/$A1AF) and the label table $FF4F. Every quantity cap is ratio_times10_capped(a, rate, cap) = min(⌊a·10/rate⌋, cap); every price is math32_muladddiv(rate, N) = ⌈rate·N/10⌉ — both CERTIFIED, so rates are stored ×10 (a rate of 15 = 1.5 gold/unit).

#ServiceCodeWhat it doesRate
1Loan$9F04borrow against town; debt ceiling = town; gold += N, debt += ⌈(loan_rate+10)·N/10⌉. So the rate is the interest: at max loan, debt rises by town−debt but you only receive (town−debt)·10/(loan_rate+10) gold (0.91→0.50/debt as the rate climbs 1→10). Treasury full → “…already full”; no collateral → “You can’t borrow”loan_rate +10

The rates are five entries of the market table ($6E0B…$6E13), re-rolled once a year (at the season wrap) by roll_period_market_rates $924A — the same table that prices Hire and Ninja (gold_men_hire_rate / hire_gold_rate) — and they also drift ±1 per transaction (cycle_economy_rate: sell rice → price down, buy rice/arms → price up, each loan → loan_rate up). So both the seasonal swing and your own trading move the price: buy-low / sell-high timing is real.

The one command you can’t always use — and that’s the point. Trade fuses three systems: a credit market (Loan/Repay — borrow against your town, the only way to spend beyond your treasury, at interest baked into loan_rate), a commodity market (Sell/Buy rice — convert between your two liquid resources at a fluctuating exchange rate), and an arms market (buy weapons for gold). Two design choices give it depth: the merchant is always in Kyoto and Sakai but only sometimes elsewhere — a concrete reason to hold the commercial capitals — and the rates change every turn, so a patient lord buys arms when they’re cheap and sells rice when it’s dear. This is why Trade gets a full subsystem page, not a thin one.
