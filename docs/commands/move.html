Move

March soldiers from one of your provinces to another — internal logistics, not an attack. Optionally ride along and relocate your daimyo with the army.

What it does

Move (driver_move $96D1) is your internal troop-logistics command — it shifts men between fiefs you already own (the destination list is deduped_owner_list, your provinces), distinct from War (attack an enemy) and Send (ship gold/rice). You pick a destination (Move where?); the capacity is min(dest.header − dest.men, source.men) — you can’t exceed the destination’s army ceiling or send more than you have. After How many men? the transfer applies through effect_move ($8CA5), which moves the men and blends the destination’s unit stats (a men-weighted average over three fields, scaled_transfer_da24) — the same anti-stacking averaging Hire uses, so pouring raw troops into a crack garrison dilutes it. The march animation plays (ui_helper_e80c(8)) and They have arrived safely.

How it works

The driver reads as a step-by-step script:

  1. $96D6Soldier check (effect_war_combat_prep_b) — no men → "You have no soldiers." and the command aborts.
  2. $96E9Select Move; the destination list is built from your own fiefs.
  3. $96FB"Move where?" — pick the destination province.
  4. $9741Capacity = min(dest.header − dest.men, source.men); zero → "That fief can’t hold more men."
  5. $9749"How many men?" → number entry (capped at capacity).
  6. $976FIf your daimyo is here ($6DA2): "Will you lead them personally?" — yes relocates the daimyo to the destination and sets its policy to Direct (province_ai_state = 5).
  7. $9798March animation 8, then "They have arrived safely."
  8. $97A7effect_move applies: men transferred, destination stats men-weighted-blended.
Three commands move things — keep them straight. Move shifts men between your fiefs; Send ships gold/rice between your fiefs; War marches men at an enemy fief. Move’s quiet power is the “lead them personally” option: it physically relocates your daimyo (clearing $6DA2 at the source, setting it at the destination) and flips the new fief to direct control — that’s how you reposition your lord to the front before a campaign, or pull him out of a fief that’s about to fall. The stat-blend on arrival means Move is for massing bodies, not preserving an elite unit’s average.
